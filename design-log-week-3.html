Theory Of Colour

Web Design

Today we looked at the theory of colour. The three areas we looked at were complementary colours, which are two colours that are on opposite sides of the colour wheel, Monochromatic colours, these are three shades, tones and tints of one base colour, and finally, analogous colours, which are three colours that are side by side on the colour wheel. We also looked at the psychology of colour. The colour red, is associated with energy, danger, strength, power, and determination. From the designers point of view, its great for grabbing attention to important information or calls to action, which really helps to get a message across and encourage quick decision making. (Cowan, K. 2015) 

By understanding the meanings of different colours and the psychology behind colours has helped me pick and choose my colours and what I want my audience to experience. I have chosen two colours along with having some black and white in there, so that there isn’t too much colour, and accessible for the person who is viewing my webpage.

Accessibility and Inclusive Design

Web Design

In todays session we learnt about accessibility and inclusive design and what is meant by this. Accessibility is the qualities that make an experience open to all, and inclusive design is a design methodology that enables and draws on the full range of human diversity. It is important to have a website that includes “inclusive design enables everyone to participate equally, confidently and independently in everyday activities” (Design Council. 2006). Additionally, we looked at a few different websites and assessed whether they were accessible. We then looked at how to make our websites more inclusive. We did this by including alt text to describe an image, alt text (alternative text) are used within HTML to describe the appearance and function of an image on a page. It is used by adding alternative text to photos, and is a principle of web accessibility. Visually impaired users using screen readers will be able to read an alt attribute to better understand an on-page image. Another way of making our websites more accessible and inclusive is by ensuing to use colours within sufficient contrast.

Having an inclusive and accessible website is very important as if it isn’t, it will be difficult to use and therefore people will not use my website. By looking and other websites and being frustrated with my experience it made me want to make my website easy to use, with not too many fonts or colours. To start with my colours weren’t contrasting enough, so I changed my background colours to a dark grey so the light pink was contrasting enough against it. This has meant that my website is accessible to the users and their experience.

Once opened, I had to check the size of the image I plan to change and change the size of the chosen image. After that, I had to export it to web legacy and change the image on Atom. Finally,  I pushed all the changes onto GitHub.
